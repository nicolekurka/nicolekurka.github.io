American Education from a Future Teacher

By: Nicole Kurka

Welcome to my website! Here we will discuss education within our American community. To start, go ahead and check out some of my blog posts. Once you finish with looking over those, take a listen to a podcast on STEM education. Finally, watch our video about college students and their needs regarding a curriculum for liberal education.

Beyond the Surface of Speaking

Blog Post 1

In this post, we take a look into the differences in linguistic features in our American schools and how we, as teachers, should approach them. It's a prominent issue, especially because so many schools vary in their level of grammatical and literary proficiency. Take a look at this post if you want to learn more about language differences in our schools!

  • Beyond the Surface of Speaking

Are We Advanced?

Blog Post 2

This post focuses on the influence of Advanced Placement classes. I, personally, benefited from AP, but I wanted to dive into the Virginia Tech student body and note the ways an AP program might have affected them. Check this post out if you want to learn more about whether your child should involve themselves in the Advanced Placement program.

  • Are We Advanced?

The Real Students of Blacksburg High School

Blog Post 3

Written in a "Humans of New York" style, this post explores the real lives and struggles of students in a Southwestern Virginia public school. If you are a teacher that wants inspiration to strengthen the relationships you create with your students, take a look at this post.

  • The Real Students of BHS

Your Kids are Suffering, but You Can Help

Blog Post 4

This last post details my experience as a teaching assistant in a local high school and how I believe that students' parents should be more involved in their academic careers. Read this post if you're starting to feel a disconnect with your child or you want to inspire your fellow parents to fully connect to their children's lives.

  • Your Kids are Suffering

Cut the Stem

Podcast

In our podcast, we discuss the effect of STEM in American schools. Many schools push curriculum supporting Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, but this leads to an overlook of more creative subjects such as art and humanities. If you are curious about the STEM wave overtaking our country, take a listen to our ten-minute podcast.

  • Cut the Stem

CLEaning Up Our Courses

Video

This video focuses on the courses that undergraduate students at Virginia Tech are required to take. In order for students to earn their degrees, they must spend at least a quarter of their time taking CLE classes, or courses that follow a Curriculum for a Liberal Education. Learn about the effectiveness of this program and the different student opinions of CLEs in this video!
